Animation

Animation is an illusion of movement, composed of a sequence of still images or frames that are shown at a certain rate, which will make the subject appear as if it is moving. This can be done through several means such as using drawings, models or digital tools.

What is 2D Animation?

2D animation is a technique that creates movement in a two-dimensional space. This involves drawing characters, objects, and backgrounds frame by frame, either traditionally by hand or by digitally using softwares such as Adobe Animate.

Softwares used for 2D Animation

  • Toon Boom Harmony
  • Flash
  • Encore
  • Adobe After Effects

Types of 2D Animation

Traditional Animation [Hand-drawn]

One of the oldest form of animation. A technique where each frame is drawn by hand, creating the illusion of movement when these frames are played in sequence. Can be done by drawing the frames individually or using cel [transparent] sheets which allows for layering.

Digital Animation

Utilizes software like Adobe Animate or Toon Boom Harmony to create and manipulate images and frames, offering efficiency and versatility.

Stop Motion Animation (2D Style)

A form of Stop-Motion, this style involves photographing real-world flat objects like cut-out paper, puppets, or illustrations, frame by frame, to simulate movement.

What is 3D Animation?

3D animation is the technique of creating moving, three-dimensional images in a digital context. These visuals are made using 3D software, allowing animators to create computerized objects that look 3D even though they're on a 2D surface.

Softwares used for 3D Animation

  • Autodesk Maya
  • Cinema 4D
  • Blender

Types of 3D Animation

Character Animation

Brings digital characters to life, making them move, speak, and express emotions. It's used in animated films, TV shows, and games.

Motion Capture (MoCap)

Motion capture uses real-life actors to record movement, which is then applied to 3D models. It is used for movies and video games.

What is Motion Graphics Animation?

Motion graphics animation combines graphic design and animation principles to create moving visual elements. It is animated graphic design, using text, shapes, and other visual elements set in motion to convey information, tell stories, or enhance a visual experience.

What are they used for?

  • Explanation Videos
  • Advertising
  • Website UX Design
  • Retail Experiences

Explanation Videos

Motion graphics is used to simplify complex ideas or processes, often seen in educational or product demo videos, where animated visuals help break down information, which make it more engaging and easier to understand.

Advertising

They often feature bold text, product visuals, and animated elements to communicate a brand message or promote a product in a dynamic and memorable way.

Social Media Content

Motion graphics enhance user experience (UX) by adding interactive or animated elements like loading animations, hover effects, or transitions between sections. These subtle animations guide users and make the site feel smoother and more intuitive.

Retail Experiences

Motion graphics are used in physical retail environments such as digital signage, interactive kiosks, or in-store screens—to draw attention, showcase products.

What is Stop Motion Animation?

Stop motion animation is an advanced flipbook-style form of animation. It involves photographing and then physically manipulating objects within your frame. As each frame is played in sequence, the technique creates the effect of an object moving itself.

How it works

Stop motion animation is created by photographing objects one frame at a time. After each photo, the animator slightly moves the object before taking the next shot. When these images are played back quickly in sequence, they create the illusion of smooth, continuous movement. This careful frame-by-frame adjustment requires patience and precision, as even small changes can make a big difference in the final animation.

Types of Stop Motion Animation

Claymation

Claymation is a technique using movable clay characters and stop-motion recording. It's a film style often seen in children's films and cartoons. It's a meticulous process, with some feature-length films taking months - or even years - to shoot.

Puppet

Puppet animation is a technique involving puppet figures that are animated frame-by-frame. Usually, the animators create a physical three-dimensional scene, similar to a small theatre, where the action will take place.

Pixilation

Pixilation is a technique in which live actors are used as a frame-by-frame subject in an animated film. Is often used as a way to blend live actors with animated ones in a movie.

Cutout

Cutout animation is a technique using flat characters, props and backgrounds cut from materials such as paper, card, stiff fabric or photographs. The props would be cut out and used as puppets for stop motion.
